What success looks like

Belong. Attend. Contribute.

Three statements that apply equally to everyone — pupils, teachers and teaching assistants, school leaders and central services staff.

1

Belong

Because you feel safe, known and included.

Pupils

You know your teacher knows you — not just your name, but what you find hard, what you're good at and what matters to you. You feel safe in your classroom and around the school. You feel included in lessons, in activities and in school life. You have at least one trusted adult. You feel you are a valued part of your school community.

Teachers & teaching assistants

You feel known by your colleagues and your leaders. Your strengths, your development needs and your contributions are recognised. You feel safe to ask questions, to admit when something isn't working and to take professional risks. You feel included in decisions that affect your work.

School leaders

You feel supported and not isolated. You have honest, trusting relationships with your DOSI, your executive team and your peer headteachers. You are willing to be honest about where you are struggling, because you trust that honesty will be met with support, not judgement.

Central services staff

You understand how your role connects to the children in our schools. You feel part of the Nova family. You have positive, trusted relationships with the schools and colleagues you support.

2

Attend and Achieve

Every day.

Pupils

You come to school every day ready to learn. Achieving means being actively involved in your learning — asking questions, responding to feedback, trying hard even when something is difficult, and making the most of every lesson and opportunity.

Teachers & teaching assistants

You come to work consistently and reliably. You bring your best to every day — preparing, delivering, reflecting. You support your colleagues as well as your pupils.

School leaders

You model the attendance and commitment you expect from others. You are present, visible, consistent and reliable. You achieve the things you have committed to and meet your responsibilities.

Central services staff

You are reliable, responsive and consistent. Schools and colleagues can depend on you to do what you said you would do, when you said you would do it, to the standard agreed.

3

Contribute Positively

To your teams and community.

Pupils

You play an active and positive part in the life of your school. You are kind to others. You take responsibility for how your behaviour affects the people around you. You contribute to making your school a better place for everyone in it.

Teachers & teaching assistants

You contribute beyond your own classroom. You share what works. You support colleagues, especially those newer or finding things harder. You contribute to networks and trust-wide improvement.

School leaders

You contribute to the Nova family beyond your own school. You open your school to other leaders, visit colleagues in other settings and bring learning back. You see yourself as a leader of the trust, not just of your school.

Central services staff

You contribute to the success of schools, not just the completion of tasks. You actively look for ways to remove friction and make it easier for schools to do their best work.

The three-year roadmap

Aligning culture, attendance and belonging

Build the foundations, embed them, then sharpen and stretch.

Year 12027–28 · Align & establish
  • Belong — Baseline belonging survey completed across all schools and all staff; inclusion standards established.
  • Attend — Trust-wide attendance strategy embedded; all schools tracking against national average with data visible to leaders.
  • Contribute — First trust-wide staff engagement survey completed; contribution networks active across the trust.
Year 22028–29 · Embed & connect
  • Belong — Belonging scores improving year on year; above regional average in the majority of schools.
  • Attend — All schools at or above national average attendance; persistent absence falling consistently.
  • Contribute — Cross-trust collaboration measurably increasing; staff recommending Nova above 80%.
Year 32029–30 · Sharpen & stretch
  • Belong — Pupil and staff belonging in top quartile nationally; every school with active inclusion evidence.
  • Attend — Above national average in every school, every phase; persistent absence below national.
  • Contribute — Staff recommending Nova above 90%; external partnerships active and Nova visible as a place people want to work.
