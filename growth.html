Nova Education Trust

Our Growth

Growth is not about getting bigger. It is about extending what works to more children, in more communities, without weakening what we already have.

Why we grow

Growth serves the mission

Every school we add should strengthen the trust, and the trust should strengthen every school that joins.

1

Reach

Transformational education for as many children as possible, extending success that is already working.

2

Social impact

A difference to local communities through better educational opportunity.

3

Sustainability

Financial resilience and the benefits that come from greater economies of scale.

4

System strength

Sharing success and supporting others, with greater opportunity for collaboration.

5

Cross-phase depth

A mixed-phase trust that provides better school-to-school support, including primary growth and trust mergers.

6

People

Professional development so staff become experts in their profession, with internal career pathways that aid recruitment and retention.

7

Better systems

The opportunity to review, strengthen and streamline systems and processes.

8

Influence

A louder voice in the system, locally, regionally and nationally.

9

Reputation

Maintaining and improving our standing as the go-to trust.

Our approach

Intelligent growth

We secure capacity ahead of need, and we take a measured, strategic approach so that quality is never traded for size.

01

Phase One: Strengthening what we have

Growth does not always mean adding schools. First, we make sure every school is operating at its full capacity, developing sixth form provision where there is room to grow, and adding pupils into year groups where schools can take them.

Where we do add schools in this phase, they will be schools that are financially viable, performing in line with or better than national expectations, judged Good or Outstanding (or close to it, with minimal support needed), and within about an hour of Nottingham.

02

Phase Two: Growing the family

We increase the number of schools and pupils in the trust, moving toward the scale at which we can act as a system leader in the region, with greater influence on the life chances of more children and greater strength as an employer.

This phase also addresses two pressures we know well: the limited and expensive specialist provision available for our most vulnerable young people, and the national and local difficulty of recruiting teachers. We will develop more inclusive provision within the trust and grow our SCITT so more teachers train with us.

03

Phase Three: Leading the system

This phase assumes our schools are performing well above national expectations and we are on track against the KPIs in our strategic plan. We open up to single academy trusts, converter academies and regional hubs, including schools that are not yet performing well, where our due diligence shows we can make a rapid difference.

We will support schools and trusts in the Department for Education’s Education Investment Areas, and we will be open to individual school transfers, sponsorship and trust mergers. Our schools already work informally in hubs; we would expand that model, letting clusters share staff, resources and expertise.

Our criteria

Who joins us

Every conversation about a new school runs through the same six questions.

1

Cultural fit

Every school keeps its own identity, but its culture has to be compatible with our mission, vision and values.

2

Geography

We prioritise schools close to our central office and our existing schools, and we are keen to build relationships with primaries that feed our secondaries.

3

Ofsted rating

We target Good and Outstanding schools first. We will consider sponsoring schools in an Ofsted category where the cultural fit is strong and the need is clear, but only where we have the capacity, or can build it, to make rapid improvement.

4

Opportunity to add capacity

Schools should gain from our central offer and bring something to ours. Every school that joins is expected to be both a giver and a receiver of support.

5

Condition of estates

We need to understand the estate risks before a school joins, so that a new school never jeopardises resources for the children and staff we already have.

6

Financial position

We assess the financial position in the context and condition of the school, making sure we can maintain quality in our current schools while extending enough resource to new ones.

Our purpose

A means, not an end

Growth is a means, not an end. We grow because more children deserve what our schools already give, and because a stronger trust makes every school in it stronger.
