Why three

The discipline is the strategy

The previous strategy had real strengths buried in it. The problem was volume — ten priorities and dozens of measures, pulling attention in every direction at once. That isn't ambition. It's diluted attention wearing ambition's clothes.

When Apple ran its annual strategy retreat, leaders would debate a list of around ten projects, then cut the bottom seven. Only the top three got any time or money. Jobs's test was subtraction: "I'm as proud of what we don't do as I am of what we do." A trust knows it has a strategy when it can say, clearly, what it will not do.

Three isn't an arbitrary tidy number, either. Working-memory research is blunt: people reliably hold about four things, often fewer — and in organisations with five stated priorities, only a quarter of managers can correctly name three of them. A strategy nobody can recall is a strategy nobody can act on.

3 enduring pillars
→ a few annual priorities
→ 3 KPIs per pillar

Onboarding a school, redefining CPL, an AP launch, the iPad rollout — these are projects, not strategy. They sit beneath the pillars and serve them, the way "launch the next iPhone" sits beneath Apple's enduring aim of making the best products. Every annual priority carries a single named owner, so accountability is never ambiguous.

Our enablers

These are not strategic priorities. They are the systems that make the strategy possible.

P

People

We recruit, develop and retain exceptional colleagues across every role.

  • We attract people who share our values and are committed to transforming lives.
  • We develop every colleague through clear pathways, honest conversations and genuine investment in their growth.
  • We retain our best people by making Nova a place where talented individuals want to build a career.
G

Governance

We provide the challenge, support and strategic direction that excellent governance requires.

  • Trustees and governors hold the trust and its schools to account, asking the right questions with access to the right information.
  • Every school has a local governing board that knows its community, understands its context and supports its leadership.
  • Governance is developmental — it builds the capacity of leaders as well as holding them to account.
£

Finance

We ensure the long-term financial sustainability of the trust and every school within it.

  • Every school operates within a balanced budget, making prudent decisions about staffing, resources and expenditure.
  • Central procurement, shared services and financial controls reduce waste and improve value for money.
  • Financial health enables ambition — we invest in the things that improve outcomes for children.
I

Infrastructure

We create safe, effective and future-ready environments in which children and adults can thrive.

  • Every building is maintained to a standard that communicates care, ambition and respect.
  • Therapeutic classroom design, Thrive provision and enhanced learning environments are infrastructure investments that directly support our strategy.
  • We plan our estate with the long term in mind, making decisions that will benefit future generations of children.
C

Compliance

We meet and exceed our statutory responsibilities, ensuring every child is safe and every school is well-governed.

  • Safeguarding is the foundation of everything. No strategic priority takes precedence over the safety of children.
  • We meet our duties as an employer, a landlord and a public body, consistently and without complacency.
  • Compliance is not a ceiling — we aim to exceed statutory requirements and to lead the sector in how we discharge our responsibilities.
T

Technology

We use technology to amplify learning, improve workflow and connect our schools and teams.

  • Our digital strategy is purposeful — technology is used because it improves outcomes, not for its own sake.
  • Microsoft Copilot, Showbie, Magma Maths, Quest Progress, Reading Progress and Kaligo are tools in service of learning and efficiency, not ends in themselves.
  • We govern AI use responsibly — encouraging confident, safe adoption rather than prohibition or uncritical use.
F

Transforming Lives Foundation

The charitable arm of Nova Education Trust, enabling us to do more for our children and communities than schools alone can achieve.

  • The Foundation funds enrichment experiences, opportunities and community projects that transform life chances for the children in our schools.
  • It ensures that disadvantage never prevents a child from accessing what our USP promises — removing financial barriers to participation.
  • Every pound raised through the Foundation is an investment in the personal development and future readiness of a Nova child.
