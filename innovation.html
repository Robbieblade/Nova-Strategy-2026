What success looks like

Growth, improvement and efficiency — for every role

Three areas where innovation shows up day to day, and what each means for the people doing the work.

1

Professional Growth

Teachers & teaching assistants

You are improving your teaching practice year on year. You engage fully with CPD. You reflect honestly on your own practice, seek feedback and act on it. You share what you learn with colleagues. You work towards ILP accreditation.

For teaching assistants specifically: you develop your skills in supporting learning, not just managing behaviour. You understand the learning goals for the children you work with. You are a co-educator, not a helper.

School leaders

You are a learner as well as a leader. You visit other schools — at least two other Nova primaries each year and one setting outside the trust. You develop other leaders around you, identifying successors and creating genuine growth opportunities.

Central services staff

You develop your expertise so that the service you provide to schools improves year on year. You share knowledge with colleagues across the trust.

Executive leaders

You drive a trust-wide culture of professional growth. You model continuous learning. You ensure the talent pipeline is active and succession planning is real in every school.

2

School Improvement

Teachers & teaching assistants

You know where your school is on its improvement journey and you understand your role in it. You engage with school self-evaluation honestly. You use data, evidence and professional dialogue to adjust your practice.

School leaders

You know your school deeply and accurately. Your self-evaluation is honest and evidence-based. You implement the GOST model with rigour. You engage fully with TSIMs, QA visits and CEO visits as developmental conversations. Therapeutic classrooms, Thrive provision and enhanced environments are school improvement decisions that sit here.

Central services & trust leaders

You support schools to improve, not manage them into compliance. Your QA processes add value, not anxiety. You connect schools to each other and to external expertise.

3

Workflow Efficiencies

Teachers & teaching assistants

Your time goes on teaching, feedback and professional development — not unnecessary administration. You use Showbie, Magma Maths and Reading Progress to make your work more efficient and effective. Marking is purposeful and proportionate.

School leaders

You protect your staff's time. You use AI tools including Microsoft Copilot to reduce administrative load. You audit your school's systems and remove things that create work without adding value.

Central services

You simplify processes rather than adding to them. Every system you introduce should make it easier for schools to do their best work. You regularly review what you ask schools to do and remove anything that does not serve children or compliance.

The three-year roadmap

From common language to exported practice

Build the foundations, embed them, then sharpen and stretch.

Year 12026–27 · Align & establish
  • Confirm the CPD pathway architecture (ILP strands, leadership, subject networks) with 100% participation and a baseline impact measure.
  • Identify a developing successor for every senior leadership role; map the gaps.
  • Move all schools onto Tracker+ and PITA so assessment data is comparable; set the digital/workflow baseline.
Year 22027–28 · Embed & connect
  • CPD pathways delivering measurable practice change; above 80% reporting impact.
  • Internal promotions rising; first cohort of identified successors stepping up.
  • ILP and digital practice consistent across rollout schools; workflow gains evidenced; AI governed and embedded for assessment and planning.
Year 32028–30 · Sharpen & stretch
  • 100% CPD participation with over 90% reporting impact; a ready successor for every senior role in every school.
  • Nova recognised regionally for its digital, workflow and AI model; schools exporting practice to other trusts.
