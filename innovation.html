What success looks like

Expertise that compounds, improvement that travels

Professional growth ▼

  • Expertise compounds across the Trust
  • Leadership capacity grows and internal talent is developed
  • Future leaders are ready before we need them

School improvement ▼

  • Research informs practice and best practice travels between schools
  • Innovation is tested safely and improvement is everyone’s job
  • Schools develop the capacity and confidence to improve themselves

Workflow & technology ▼

  • Technology makes learning clearer and more accessible
  • Administrative drag falls and staff time moves to where it matters
  • Systems become simpler, safer and easier to use

The three-year roadmap

From common language to exported practice

Build the foundations, embed them, then sharpen and stretch.

Year 12027–28 · Align & establish
  • Professional Growth — CPD pathway architecture confirmed, all staff enrolled and baseline impact data collected.
  • School Improvement — Self Evaluation Toolkit embedded in all schools; successor audit complete and gaps mapped.
  • Workflow Efficiencies — Baseline administrative workload established; digital tools consistently used across all schools.
Year 22028–29 · Embed & connect
  • Professional Growth — 80%+ staff reporting CPD improves practice; first Nova Advanced Skills Teachers accredited.
  • School Improvement — All schools with identified successors stepping into development roles; internal promotions rising.
  • Workflow Efficiencies — Measurable reduction in admin load evidenced; Microsoft Copilot and AI tools governed and embedded across the trust.
Year 32029–30 · Sharpen & stretch
  • Professional Growth — 90%+ staff reporting CPD impact; Nova recognised regionally for its professional development model.
  • School Improvement — Every senior role has a ready successor; schools exporting practice to other trusts.
  • Workflow Efficiencies — Administrative workload well below baseline; staff time consistently redirected to high-impact work.
