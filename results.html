Measures

Three KPIs — our three-year targets

The point of all of it. We track the whole-child entitlement as closely as we track reading and maths — if something is a promise, it’s a promise we can evidence.

KPI 1

Ofsted evaluation areas

No school in an Urgent Improvement category; a growing share in the top bands; safeguarding consistently Met.
KPI 2

Pupil outcomes

Above national for attainment and progress, primary and secondary, with gaps narrowing year on year.
KPI 3

Disadvantaged & SEND outcomes

Above national for similar pupils, with the gap to peers closing across the journey.
The three-year roadmap

From clean baselines to distinctive outcomes

Build the foundations, embed them, then sharpen and stretch.

Year 12026–27 · Align & establish
  • Align every school SEF and SIP to the six Ofsted evaluation areas using the Nova Self-Evaluation Tool; agree 2–4 priorities per school via GOST.
  • Confirm aspirational outcome targets per school against national and similar-pupil benchmarks.
  • Establish the disadvantaged and SEND gap baseline in every school.
Year 22027–28 · Embed & connect
  • Outcomes above national in the majority of schools; disadvantaged and SEND gaps measurably narrower than Year 1.
  • All schools secure or improving across Ofsted areas in QA; mock inspections rehearsed against the toolkit.
  • USP entitlement tracked for every child, linked to behaviour, attendance and achievement.
Year 32028–30 · Sharpen & stretch
  • Outcomes above national with gaps closing across the primary and secondary journey; disadvantaged and SEND above national for similar pupils.
  • No school with an Urgent Improvement area; a growing share in the top Ofsted bands.
  • The Trust positioned as high-quality against the DfE’s five pillars, with the capacity and credibility to grow.
