Three KPIs — our three-year targets

The point of all of it. We track the whole-child entitlement as closely as we track reading and maths — if something is a promise, it’s a promise we can evidence.

Attainment and progress

KPI 2 — Personal Development

Enrichment and wellbeing

100% of pupils accessing their full USP enrichment entitlement. Pupil wellbeing above national average — tracked, evidenced and acted upon.
KPI 3 — Future Readiness

Destinations and readiness

Positive destinations above national average for all groups and all phases. Work-related learning experiences in place for all secondary pupils.

The three-year roadmap

From clean baselines to distinctive outcomes

Build the foundations, embed them, then sharpen and stretch.

Year 12027–28 · Align & establish
  • Achievement — All schools aligned to the Self Evaluation Toolkit; disadvantaged and SEND gap baselines established in every school.
  • Personal Development — Enrichment entitlement mapped across all schools; USP tracking in place for every child.
  • Future Readiness — Positive destinations baseline established for all phases; careers education programme in place across all secondary schools.
Year 22028–29 · Embed & connect
  • Achievement — Attainment above national in the majority of schools; disadvantaged and SEND gaps measurably narrower year on year.
  • Personal Development — 80%+ of pupils accessing full enrichment entitlement; wellbeing data improving and acted upon.
  • Future Readiness — Positive destinations above regional average; work-related learning in place across all secondary phases.
Year 32029–30 · Sharpen & stretch
  • Achievement — Attainment and progress above national across all phases; disadvantaged pupils exceeding statistical neighbours.
  • Personal Development — 100% of pupils accessing full USP entitlement; pupil wellbeing above national average.
  • Future Readiness — Positive destinations above national average for all groups and all phases.
Where this sits

Achievement and personal development & wellbeing

Results speaks to the Ofsted areas of achievement and personal development & wellbeing, and underpins every other area. It delivers the DfE high-quality and inclusive education pillar.

EYFS & KS1

Achievement — Results

Strong early reading and number foundations — phonics, early mathematics and oral language secure; children meeting expected standards at the end of KS1.

Disadvantaged children making strong progress from entry — gaps tracked from the start, closing year on year, and outcomes exceeding statistical neighbours when context is taken into account.

SEND children making strong progress against their targets and on their curriculum pathway, with provision well matched to need.

KS2

Achievement — Results

Attainment at expected and greater depth standards in reading, writing and mathematics — above national average and improving year on year.

Disadvantaged pupils' outcomes exceed statistical neighbours when context is taken into account; gaps close year on year throughout the key stage.

SEND pupils making strong progress on their curriculum pathway and meeting their individual targets, supported by well-matched provision.

KS3 & KS4

Achievement — Results

Attainment and progress above national averages at GCSE across all subjects — with no school in the bottom quartile nationally.

Disadvantaged pupils' outcomes exceed statistical neighbours when context and starting points are taken into account; gaps close year on year.

SEND pupils achieve against their targets and on pathways that reflect both their potential and their needs — no gap that is not understood, owned and being addressed.

KS5

Achievement — Results

A-level and vocational results above national averages, with students achieving grades that reflect their potential not their starting point.

Disadvantaged students achieving outcomes that match their ability — gaps closing year on year, with no ceiling on aspiration.

SEND students accessing and succeeding in the full range of post-16 pathways; all students achieving the qualifications they need for the next step.

EYFS & KS1

Personal Development — Results

Every child accessing the full Nova USP enrichment entitlement — no child left out because of cost or circumstance.

Strong character foundations developing — kindness, resilience, curiosity and a love of learning that children carry forward.

Wellbeing tracked and supported; every child feeling safe, known and confident to engage with the world.

KS2

Personal Development — Results

All pupils engaged in the full breadth of USP enrichment activities — sports, arts, performance, community — with no barriers to participation.

Character and values embedded — pupils showing courage, kindness and responsibility in daily life, with leadership opportunities for all.

Positive wellbeing evidenced through school surveys; concerns identified early and supported consistently.

KS3 & KS4

Personal Development — Results

All pupils accessing their full USP entitlement — including those who face the greatest barriers.

Strong personal development evident in character, leadership, community involvement and cultural capital — every pupil known as a whole person, not only an exam candidate.

Wellbeing supported through a whole-school approach; mental health taken seriously and acted upon, not treated as an afterthought.

KS5

Personal Development — Results

Students equipped for life beyond school — confident, articulate, with a record of achievement and service they can speak to.

Wellbeing supported through a sixth form culture that prepares students for independence, resilience and the demands of what comes next.

All students leaving with a strong sense of who they are and what they can offer the world.

EYFS & KS1

Future Readiness — Results

Children ready for the next stage of education — socially, emotionally and academically — with strong foundations in literacy and mathematics.

Curiosity, confidence and a love of learning that will sustain children through everything that follows.

Every child known and supported as they transition, so no child starts their next stage without the skills, relationships or belief they need.

KS2

Future Readiness — Results

Pupils ready for secondary school — with secure literacy and numeracy, study skills, self-regulation and the resilience to manage a new environment.

Pupils who believe in their own potential and understand that their future is shaped by their choices now.

A pupil record of achievement and enrichment that reflects the whole child and supports a confident transition.

KS3 & KS4

Future Readiness — Results

Positive destinations for all — apprenticeships, further education, sixth form, employment — with disadvantaged pupils achieving positive destinations above national average.

Careers education, information and guidance that is honest, ambitious and personalised; every pupil with work-related learning experiences.

Every pupil with a plan they believe in and the qualifications to support it.

KS5

Future Readiness — Results

University, apprenticeship and employment destinations that reflect ambition and preparation, not just attainment.

Disadvantaged students supported to access competitive destinations — no ceiling on aspiration, and outcomes that match ability not background.

Every student leaving with a positive next step, an application that tells their story, and the confidence to take it.
