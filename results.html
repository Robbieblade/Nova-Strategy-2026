What success looks like

Achievement, character and readiness — by phase

Results are entirely focused on children. Each of the three areas is broken down by phase, from Early Years to Sixth Form.

1

Achievement

EYFS & KS1

Children leave Early Years with Good Level of Development including strong communication, language and literacy foundations and early number sense. By end of Year 1, the vast majority pass the Phonics Screening Check. By end of KS1, children are reading confidently, writing with growing independence and using number fluently. Gaps are narrower at the end of KS1 than at entry.

KS2

Children leave primary school with secure skills in reading, writing and mathematics. Combined RWM attainment is at or above national with positive progress scores. Disadvantaged pupils achieve at or above national averages for similar pupils. The attainment gap between disadvantaged and non-disadvantaged pupils is narrower at the end of KS2 than at the start.

KS3 & KS4

Students make strong progress from KS2 starting points. Attainment at 4+ and 5+ English and Maths is above national average and above the line of best fit for schools with similar deprivation levels. No school sits in the bottom quartile nationally.

KS5

Students progress to destinations that match their ability and ambition. A Level points per entry improve year on year. Value Added scores are positive across academic and applied pathways.

2

Personal Development

EYFS & KS1

Children develop the foundations of self-regulation. They are curious. They play well with others. They know how to be a good friend. They have a growing sense of their own identity and take pride in their school.

KS2

Children develop resilience, integrity and responsibility. They contribute to the life of their school. They have had at least 11 meaningful enrichment experiences by the time they leave primary (11 by 11). They develop oracy — listening carefully, reasoning out loud and speaking with growing confidence.

KS3 & KS4

Students develop strong character alongside academic qualifications. They contribute to school communities, take on leadership roles and engage in community projects. By 16, every student has had experiences that have genuinely broadened their horizons.

KS5

Students develop the independence, judgement and self-awareness needed for life beyond school. They leave with confidence in who they are and what they are capable of.

3

Future Readiness

EYFS & KS1

Children are ready for the next stage of their education. They have the communication skills, early literacy and numeracy foundations and social and emotional skills to access KS1 and beyond. They are curious about the world and motivated to learn.

KS2

Children are ready for secondary school. They have secure literacy and numeracy skills. They have the study skills, self-regulation and resilience to manage an increased workload and a new environment. They have a sense of their own strengths and interests.

KS3 & KS4

Students are ready for the next stage of education, training or employment. They have made genuine choices about subjects, pathways and futures. They have work-related learning experiences and have met employers. SEND pupils have destinations that reflect their potential.

KS5

Students leave Nova with clear, positive destinations. They progress into higher education, apprenticeships, employment or further training that aligns with their strengths and ambitions.

The three-year roadmap

From clean baselines to distinctive outcomes

Build the foundations, embed them, then sharpen and stretch.

Year 12026–27 · Align & establish
  • Align every school SEF and SIP to the six Ofsted evaluation areas using the Nova Self-Evaluation Tool; agree 2–4 priorities per school via GOST.
  • Confirm aspirational outcome targets per school against national and similar-pupil benchmarks.
  • Establish the disadvantaged and SEND gap baseline in every school.
Year 22027–28 · Embed & connect
  • Outcomes above national in the majority of schools; disadvantaged and SEND gaps measurably narrower than Year 1.
  • All schools secure or improving across Ofsted areas in QA; mock inspections rehearsed against the toolkit.
  • USP entitlement tracked for every child, linked to behaviour, attendance and achievement.
Year 32028–30 · Sharpen & stretch
  • Outcomes above national with gaps closing across the primary and secondary journey; disadvantaged and SEND above national for similar pupils.
  • No school with an Urgent Improvement area; a growing share in the top Ofsted bands.
  • The Trust positioned as high-quality against the DfE’s five pillars, with the capacity and credibility to grow.
